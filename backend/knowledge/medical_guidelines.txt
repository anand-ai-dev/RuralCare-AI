FEVER

High fever above 102°F requires close monitoring.
Drink plenty of fluids.
Take paracetamol if appropriate.
Persistent fever over 3 days requires medical evaluation.

------------------------

CHEST PAIN

Chest pain with sweating, pain radiating to the left arm,
difficulty breathing or nausea is a medical emergency.
Call emergency services immediately.

------------------------

COUGH

Persistent cough with fever may indicate pneumonia.
Difficulty breathing requires urgent evaluation.

------------------------

BREATHING

Difficulty breathing, wheezing, or bluish lips are emergency symptoms.
Immediate medical attention is recommended.

------------------------

DEHYDRATION

Drink Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS).
Monitor urine output.
Severe dehydration requires hospital treatment.

------------------------

DIABETES

People with diabetes and infection have a higher risk of complications.
Monitor blood sugar regularly.

------------------------

HYPERTENSION

Very high blood pressure with headache, blurred vision, or chest pain
requires emergency medical care.

------------------------

BLEEDING

Apply direct pressure to the wound.
Elevate the injured limb if possible.
Seek emergency care if bleeding is severe.

------------------------

BURNS

Cool the burn under running water for at least 20 minutes.
Do not apply toothpaste, butter, or oil.

------------------------

STROKE

Face drooping.
Arm weakness.
Speech difficulty.

Call emergency medical services immediately.

------------------------

HEART ATTACK

Symptoms include chest pain, sweating, shortness of breath,
pain radiating to the left arm or jaw.

Immediate hospital treatment is required.
